Why Does Water Have an Expiration Date?

An article by Tiffanie Chen

Explanation of Expiration

The necessity to have an expiration date on water bottles began in New Jersey in 1987 when a state law was passed that required all food and beverages to have an expiration date. The expiration date is determined by two years after the bottle of water has been manufactured. It is there to contribute to keeping people healthy. While the actual water won’t cause the body any harm, the plastic bottle the water comes in is what causes damage. The plastic that water is packaged in is usually made out of polyethylene terephthalate (PET) is somewhat permeable-- having small spaces/holes that allow air or liquid to pass through. This causes the water to absorb in scents from the bottles’ surroundings leading to a change in taste.

Long Term Effects of Plastic on Water

Over time the polyethylene terephthalate will start to leak into the water. The large packs of pre-packaged water bought by consumers usually are drinkable up till at least a year. After one year, the taste will be slightly different. Out of all the variety of plastic available to produce water bottles, the best type is polyethylene terephthalate because it is known that the chances of leaking chemicals to be very low. Leaving a water bottle out in high temperatures will also cause the chemicals from the bottles to leak into the water. Here is a list of all known plastics to be used to make water bottles.

Materials Plastic is Made of:

  1. Polyethylene Terephthalate
  2. High Density Polyethylene
  3. Polyvinyl Chloride
  4. Low Density Polyethylene
  5. Polypropylene
  6. Polystyrene
  7. Polycarbonate
