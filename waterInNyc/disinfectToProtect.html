Disinfect To Protect

An article by Ting Ting Zhang

Introduction to Water Purification

Early in history, people drank water from rivers, oceans, ponds, and bodies of water that contained unwanted compounds, debris, and bacteria from inorganic and organic materials which are often found in water when specific contaminants are not removed from the purifying process of H2O. In result, individuals contracted viruses, diseases, and infections that led to death and cancer. Today, corporations purify water for society to drink and function on. The necessary equipment for water purification is expensive and the process itself is difficult to execute. With simple and inexpensive materials, homeowners or campers can purify water with different methods. The most common and effective water purification is boiling. Boiling is the cheapest and safest method to purify water. The process can also kill germs and parasites and although not visible to naked eye, the effect of water boiling is super effective.

Materials

Heat source (camping stove, cooker, etc.)
Vessel (to hold the water)

Procedure

Pour the water into a vessel and turn on the fire to the highest setting.
When the water boils, you will see bubbles forming and fast movement of water.
Wait around 1-3 minutes in order to purify the water and give it time to kill off the germs. (Remember: The more water you have, the longer it should boil, as there are more unwanted contaminants.) Considerations If the water is cloudy, then the water must be filtered before the boiling process because you do not want to ingest the debris.

More Methods of Purification

Filtration involves the separation of a mixture where small solid particles are in liquid or air. The water that is filtered is called the filtrate. Filtration is one of the most efficient ways to decontaminate water and make it safe for human consumption. The process eliminates both large and small compounds and dangerous contaminants that cause diseases. Settling Another common separation process is called distillation. Distillation uses boiling to separate mixtures of liquid solutions. Take into account that different substances in the mixture will have different boiling points.



Materials for Filtration

Coffee filter paper or strand of cloth
Bottle (or anything to hold water)

Materials for Settling

Container
Lid
Rainwater
Jugs (not necessary)

Procedure for Filtration

Insert the inner part of the coffee filter paper or strand of cloth into the opening of the water holder.
Carefully pour water into the opening with the filter paper or cloth inside. While doing this, make sure to hold the paper/cloth around the opening so when the water is being poured, the cloth/paper does not fall in with the water.
Remove filter paper or cloth and drink the water.

Procedure for Settling

Put a container outside when raining to collect the rainwater.
Put the lid on the container after it has stopped raining.
Leave the container outside for 2 days in order for the unclean minerals to settle.
Pour water into a clean jug for later use if needed, this is called decanting, as you are leaving the sediments behind.
