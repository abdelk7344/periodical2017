Why So Much Water?

An article by Justin Kong

Why is Water Important?

Have you ever wondered why and how much water there is in our bodies? Well our bodies are made up of 60% water. Our bodies require water to aid our systems in our bodies to keep it running. If you learn the facts of the different ways water is used in our bodies and the amount of uses it has, you will recognize why our bodies have so much water. Water that is drinkable contains electrolytes so it helps your cell function properly. Electrolytes carry an electrical charge for muscle contractions. There is a reason why our bodies have 60% water so our nervous system and muscles can function. However 60% is just an average because this number varies depending on the age of the person. The range of water in our bodies is 60%-70% so more or less will be fatal. You will come to recognize the importance of water and drink more.

Why 60% Water in Our Bodies

If you are one of those people whose life revolves around food, you might not realize it shouldn’t be prioritized over H2O. Believe me, I did my research and this is 100% true. Water plays an important role in our bodies starting from our tiny human cells to our entire digestive system. There is a reason why our bodies are made up of 60% water.
Cells: Let’s start small and focus on how water helps our cells regulate and undergo cell division. Down to the basics of biology, cells hold waste, food, and nutrients such as water in the vacuoles; which is used within the cell to help the cell function. Given that water is in our cells, drinking healthy amounts of water will help keep our integumentary system lively.
Integumentary system: The integumentary system consists of hair, nails, and skin. The skin is our largest organ so keeping it moisturized can only be accomplished with water. Given that it is our largest organ with water within its cells, contributes to why we are made up 60% water.
Body temperature: Water helps our bodies maintain a stable body temperature of 98.6°F through perspiration. When our bodies are hot, we sweat to release heat to maintain the optimal temperature to allow our bodies to function correctly and smoothly. This is possible because water has a large heat capacity so it limits change in body temperature.
Digestion:Water also helps our digestion because it helps break down the soluble fibers that we consume. Saliva consists of water so consuming water will help your body produce saliva to aid in digestion. With all this need for water in our bodies it is blatantly obvious why our bodies are 60% water.
