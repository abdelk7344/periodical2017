What is Dehydration?

An article by Joshua Louis

Effects of Dehydration

Water is the best thing you can have. You don’t only need it, it's refreshing, but what will happen if you become dehydrated? Well, don't worry 75% of people are dehydrated on average. First, you need to be aware of what dehydration is before we can tell you the consequences. Dehydration is decline due to the amount of water in your body. Just 1% decline of water in the body will result in dehydration and the longer you go without water the more harm to your body. You can also experience a decline in exercise performance, “when an individual is dehydrated by as little as 2% of body weight. Losses in excess of 5% of body weight can decrease the capacity for work by about 30%” according to Asker Jeukendrup.

Causes & Effects

Causes of Dehydration for Adults

  • Heat Exposure
  • Vomiting, Diarrhea, and Increased Urination Due to Infection
  • Diseases Such as Diabetes
  • The Inability to Seek Appropriate Water and Food
  • An Impaired Ability to Drink
  • No Access to Safe Water

Symptoms of Dehydration

  • Increased Thirst
  • Dry Mouth and Swollen Tongue
  • Weakness
  • Dizziness
  • Palpitation
  • Fainting
  • Inability to Sweat

When to go to the Hospital

  • Fever Higher than 103 Degrees Fahrenheit
  • Confusion
  • Sluggishness
  • Headache
  • Seizures
  • Difficulty Breathing
  • Chest or Abdominal Pains
  • Fainting
  • No Urine in the Last 12 Hours

So let’s recap. You now know the causes and effects, symptoms, and when to get medical help. Dehydration is a crisis many countries have. According to Henry Hank Jones , “even though England has access to good drinking water, it has recorded over 2000 deaths due to dehydration. Dehydration contributes to over 1.5 millions death every year, 42000 deaths every week.” This shows that dehydration is a problem not to be taken lightly and is very important. Stay hydrated and remember nothing can substitute water.
