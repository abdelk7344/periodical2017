Melting Glaciers

An article by Bernice Shi

Glaciers Retreat

A glacier is a huge mass of ice that moves slowly towards land. They are made of fallen snow that compiles over years. The fallen snow remains in one place and starts to become ice. After they compress into a large ice mass, it thickens, and becomes a glacier. The glaciers currently take up 10% of the earth’s land. However, as time passes, glaciers have been melting away due to global warming. Glaciers melting away can cause huge harms to the world.

What Would Happen to the Earth if All the Glaciers Melt Away?

  • When glaciers melt it will make the ocean water less salty
  • Marine animals cannot adjust to the change in saltiness and rise of temperature of the ocean
  • Ocean currents that regulate temperature will be affected
  • Energy constrained in the Earth’s crust and trigger massive earthquakes
  • The polar bears and the arctic animals that live in the north pole would be gone. Sea levels will rise 210 feet
  • The Atlantic seaboard in North America would vanish
  • The land of South America will continue to be wiped out
  • The rise of temperature will make Africa a dry land
  • Land in Europe will be gone due to the rise of sea level
  • Land in Asia would flood
  • Australia will become a desert
  • Antarctica would not be able to survive

Did You Know?

  • Glaciers contain 70% of Earth's fresh water
  • The largest glacier on Earth is 60 miles wide and around 270 miles Long
  • There are around 100,000 glaciers in Alaska
  • They are formed by snowflakes
  • Glaciers can form overactive volcanoes
  • Glaciers appear to be blue sometimes when they are dense. They absorb the red and yellow lights and reflect the blue lights
  • Alpine glaciers move downwards from mountaintop to valleys
  • Continental glaciers flatten anything in their way
