What is water?

An article by ZiYu Zhuo

What do we Know About Water?

According to Wikipedia, “water is a transparent and nearly colorless chemical substance that is the main constituent of Earth's streams, lakes, and oceans and the fluids of most living organisms. Its chemical formula, H2O, means that its molecule contains one oxygen and two hydrogen atoms that are connected by covalent bonds, but we don’t need to understand every part of it. We don’t care that water is also called H2O or it contains two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom, but we do need to know the effect of it’s internal molecule and how it interacts with other substances. Water has three phases: Solid, Liquid, and gas.Different phases have different properties. The most common phase of water that people see is liquid water, like drinking water and other uses. Water is clear and the most abundant substance in the world.

Water Molecule in Chemistry

Water is a tiny bent molecule with the molecular formula H2O, because of how its atoms bond together and interact with each other, it has special properties. Water molecule is polar because its structure is not symmetrical. Water can easily dissolve polar substances because it is polar. The two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom share electrons and are bonded with covalent bond. Water molecule looks like a reverse V with the oxygen atoms on the turning point and two hydrogen atoms at the two ends. Water has two different charge. the oxygen side is negative, while the hydrogen side is positive and each side is attracted to their opposite charge. All these properties result in a strong intermolecular force because of hydrogen bonding. Hydrogen bonding is the attraction force between polar substance when a hydrogen atom covalently bond with other high electronegative atom, like oxygen, fluorine and etc.
